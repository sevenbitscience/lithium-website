Overview

Lithium is a soft silvery metal that reacts with water.

Lithium has an atomic number of 3, an atomic mass of 6.941, and is repersented on the periodic table by the symbol 'Li'.

Lithium metal is not present in nature, however small amounts of lithium are a part of most rocks.

Discovery

Petalite, a mineral containing lithium, was discovered in the 1700's by Jozé Bonifácio de Andralda e Silva. However, lithium metal was not identified until 1817, by Johan August Arfvedson. Arfvedson noted that petalite gave off a red flame when placed in a fire and concluded that it contained a previously unknown metal. Arfvedson was not able to seperate the lithium from the mineral

In 1821 William Brande obtained a small amount of lithium and in 1855 chemists Robert Bunsen and Augustus Matthiessen were able to produce lithium in bulk.

Uses

Lithium is widely used in rechargeable batteries for a wide range of electronic devices
