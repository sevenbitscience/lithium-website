Overview

Lithium is a soft silvery metal that reacts with water.

Lithium has an atomic number of 3, an atomic mass of 6.941, and is repersented on the periodic table by the symbol 'Li'.

Pure lithium metal is not present in nature, however it small amounts of lithium are a part of almost all rocks.

Discovery

Lithium was discovered in 1817 by Johan August Arfvedson.
